I just got a new paper published in the Review of Symbolic Logic:

https://doi.org/10.1017/S1755020323000199

Basically, the paper revisits the analytic-synthetic distinction in constructive type theory. I discuss certain problems with Martin-Löf's claim that a : A and a = b : A are analytic forms of judgment and propose and defend an alternative view according to which a judgment is analytic when its correctness is entirely a consequence of the evaluation of the expressions that occur in it. This means that some judgments of the forms a : A and a = b : A are synthetic!
