@damonyoung @Vinny787 that IS the imperative (prepara), with a pronoun (te) tacked on. Positive imperatives always put the pronoun (if there is one) tacked on at the end of the word. The accent mark is added to keep the same syllable stressed as before.

Example imperative forms:
Prepare: prepara
Prepare yourself: prepárate
Prepare me: prepárame
Don´t prepare yourself: no te prepare

(note on the negative the pronoun goes before, and the verb changes to its present subjunctive form, ie a->e)
