Juan R. Loaiza

@juanrloaiza@hcommons.social

@ninokadic I agree with you that Ubuntu should not be recommended as The Beginner Distro over others (like Mint). But I do think your arguments show precisely why there is indeed a distinction to be made between "beginner" (or "beginner-friendly(ier)") distros and more advanced ones. Some distros offer more configuration out-of-the-box with less choices required. For knowledgeable users, this is bad and "bloated". But for learning users, it's better and avoids being overwhelmed. There's also maintenance costs: Arch and other rolling distros tend to be more difficult to maintain (unless you know what you're doing) than other distros, again at the cost of accepting decisions made by the distro developers and not the user.

In any case, I use and recommend Fedora. I consider myself an intermediate user, I've also been on Ubuntu, Arch, Manjaro, and Debian, and Fedora is the one I've enjoyed the most.
