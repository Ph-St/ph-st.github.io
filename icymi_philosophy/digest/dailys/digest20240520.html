climate dodged again

#haiku #senryu #poem #ShortPoem #climate #media #journalism #priorities

- - - - -
Notes
- - - - -

I speak often of a "euphoria", like in the effect of hypoxia, where one becomes enthralled with some trivial action because all oxygen is gone and there is no capacity to reach other topics, even those that might be critical.

When I say media is euphoric that an event like a celebrity or politician dying, or a school shooting happening, I don't necessarily mean the individual people are happy. But I do recall someone [1] saying that what it means in the brain to be happy doing something is for that thing to have captured the brain's processor, and all other things one might be doing with the brain are quietly miserable as they await a chance to have control again.

The media pats itself on the back when it covers familiar things, things for which it has a script. They knew who to call, what video to pull up. They practically have it all written. It's smooth. They are hyper-competent at doing this thing.

Whether or not it is in fact important in the pecking order of what the world faces.

A school shooting affects hundreds of people directly, perhaps thousands in family indirectly, and many others looking in because of the shock they feel as media fixates. And these things recur.

I don't wish at all to say these things don't matter. But I do mean to comment objectively on scale.

A world leader dying can affect a nation, perhaps even create ripples into other nations and their politics.

Climate already affects millions around the planet in all countries in an enduring and expanding way. There is no escape from climate's effect, even ultimately from its danger. Not for anyone on the planet, present or future. Ultimately all the billions on the planet will be profoundly affected, put at risk, perhaps even completely killed.

Rather than think I am trivializing these other things, instead think I am using these things that we see as bad to say Climate is EVEN worse, even more broad in people affected, even more hurtful, even more enduring, likely affecting humanity for thousands of years, if we survive it at all, which is far from a certainty.

But we are not practiced at covering climate. And anyway, it is not news. It's the same as yesterday. Our media algorithm is driven by novelty, not importance.

So we cover things that have the syntactic appearance of importance, even as their ultimate impact on humanity is orders of magnitude less.

Media enters a euphoria, locking out all else. It is happy, if not with the event itself, at least with how competent it feels about its coverage of these topics.

Media's pride in craftsmanship on these things of familiar structure is clear. It spins out commercials saying this is the place to turn the dial to get the best coverage of such things.

It would be less happy covering climate because it would be less certain of its competence and would not want to admit that.

So another day passes without the "need" to mention climate. Instead, today will be wall-to-wall coverage of the death of a world leader.

What, after all, is one day lost about climate? Well, they really don't know what the effect of that is because they do not have a honed competence on that.. It's too hard to cover. Too not new. Too uninteresting.

Hypoxic euphoria also has the sense of making us stupid at a critical time, risking death through acts of poor judgment. I like that part of that metaphor as well. It fits well here. Even if it is crushingly sad.

-----Footnotes---
[1] I'm thinking this memory of happiness as a commandeering of the mental processor is an idea of Minsky from Society of Mind, but I might be misremembering. If anyone has a clearer recollection, please attach it here.
