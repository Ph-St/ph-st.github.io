I'm still working through Marx's Capital Vol. 1 Chapter 15. It's a long one.

Marx notes how machines can make work easier for workers but that doesn't make their lives easier. The work seems easy so workers are less resistant to working longer hours. But this means more time spent on repetitive tasks that enrich the capitalist and less time for themselves. Minimal physical strain but the psychic strain is incalculable.
