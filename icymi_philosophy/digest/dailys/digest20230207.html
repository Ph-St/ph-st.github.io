Me:

If my sister was half my age when I was 4, how old was she when I was 30?

ChatGPT:

Let's call your sister's age at the time you were 4 "x". So x + 4 = 4 and x = 0. If you are 30 now, then your sister is 30/2 = 15 years old.

- - - - - - - - - -

Hmmm.

I think I would have accepted 28 or 41, given a minor ambiguity in how I phrased the question.

It's good that it shows its work, but it's bad that it can't do simple plausibility checking to figure out that it's way off.

Interesting that it made a working assumption about my current age, and it sort of makes sense that it might think her age was initially 15 when I was 4 (which would lead to the 41 result). But that it's 15 now is weird. I guess many students go through this kind of floundering.

It's unfortunate that it has to be "trained" and I can't just tell it and have it "learn". You'd think learning was training, but I suppose there's a trust issue in there. Surprisingly many things in the world have trust involved where I never knew.

I've gotten other bad answers out of it where it just says the answer and it's just wrong and there's no hint that it's worried it is wrong, that it doesn't understand, etc. That bothers me a lot.

In the end, this is one more example of why we should not rush to trust it on critical tasks.
