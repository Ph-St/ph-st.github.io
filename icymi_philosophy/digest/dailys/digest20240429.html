I was saddened to hear that Charles Parsons (1933-2024), the philosopher of mathematics, has died.

His 1965 paper "Frege's theory of numbers" re-inforced my early fascination with logicism, and I've been reading Parsons with great admiration on and off ever since.

I had the pleasure of reviewing his 2008 Big Book, *Mathematical Thought and Its Objects* -- OK, I was unpersuaded by some of his major claims, but that's philosophy for you. Here's the review (short version): https://www.logicmatters.net/resources/pdfs/ParsonsReview.pdf

But that review really only touches on two of his themes, and there's much more to wrestle with. I battled at length with the twists and turns of his book here, learning (I hope!) a lot: https://www.logicmatters.net/resources/pdfs/ParsonsLongReview.pdf
