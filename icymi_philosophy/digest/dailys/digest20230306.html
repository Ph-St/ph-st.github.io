When did passenger air travel to/from Australia overtake sea travel? It’s surprisingly hard to get a definitive answer: Blainey, Tyranny of Distance, says “by the mid 1960s”; Lee, Transport, implies it wasn’t until the 747 era (i.e. the 1970s).

The ABS to the rescue! In 1957 short-term visitors from overseas already hugely favoured air (air: 75k, sea: 44k), though their Australian equivalents still just preferred sea (air: 51k, sea: 58k). Australians didn’t definitively switch to air until 1958 (air: 61k, sea: 57k).

So it seems safe to say the switch took place “by the late 1950s”, then. *But* these numbers exclude permanent migration, which was of course huge but for which I can’t find an air/sea breakdown.
