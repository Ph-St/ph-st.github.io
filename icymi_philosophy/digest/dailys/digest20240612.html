Andrei A. Klishin

@aklishin@fediphilosophy.org

Lynn Conway passed away the other day. Many words have been said elsewhere about her remarkable career achievements as an engineer, educator, and activist but I wanted to share a brief personal anecdote.
We had a meeting at University of Michigan early in my PhD circa 2017 to talk about design problems. She was very excited that someone finally gets that the Mead-Conway geometric rules are inequalities and thus allow for many solutions with a given topology of necessary transistor connections. We sadly never got around to computing the entropy of VLSI layouts, but this acknowledgement was hugely inspirational.
She also formulated the geometric rules in terms of a fundamental lengthscale lambda that kept shrinking over decades due to Moore's law, but the rules themselves apply today as they did in 1979. So if you hear someone in science talk about "scale free design rules" (common in nanoscience and colloid studies), Lynn Conway did it first back in the day! What a legend!
