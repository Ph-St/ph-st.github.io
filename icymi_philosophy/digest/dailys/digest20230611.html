Faith and Fate: Limits of Transformers on Compositionality
https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.18654

Paper examines Tranformers in compositional tasks that require combining basic reasoning operations to follow computational paths that arrive at unique correct solution.

Takeways:

Single-step reasoning is accurate. Planning and combining multiple steps for correct overall reasoning is difficult.

Near-perfect performance on in-distribution, but fails entirely in generalizing.

Transformers solve compositional tasks by reducing multi-step compositional reasoning into linearized subgraph matching, without necessarily developing systematic problem-solving skills.

Having seen significant portions of the required computation graph during training can result seemingly impressive performance.

Autoregressive characteristic of
Transformers forces them to tackle problems sequentially. It presents a fundamental challenge that cannot be resolved by instructing the model to generate a step-by-step solution
