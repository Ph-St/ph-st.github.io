Rousseau wants to find a source of political obligation that’s compatible with equality and moral agency, & which doesn’t rely upon controversial metaphysical claims. Since we’re equal, nobody can have a natural right to rule; & since we are free, we mustn’t simply obey the commands of another. Thus, we remain free and equal by writing the laws that govern us ourselves: we make ourselves part of a collective & the collective decides. Only laws made collectively are legitimate.
