More evidence that time pressure inhibits reflective thinking:

Covariance detection tasks lure people into judging the effectiveness of an intervention by the NUMBER of times it worked rather than the RATIO of times it worked (compared to the times is didn't work).

As with other reflection tests, a time limit reduced performance on the task (N ≅ 400).

Brian Weatherson

@bweatherson@fediphilosophy.org

I've been playing around with citation data to get a sense of what philosophers at various times thought was the most important recent work being done. Some results are in the post linked below. I'll do a brief summary here.

https://brian.weatherson.org/quarto/posts/citations-top-ten/top-ten.html

Imagine some researchers at different times, with perfect, up-to-date, citation information.

Each of them ask "What is the most cited ever philosophy journal article that's less than ten years old?"

The results are in the graphics I've attached.

I'm just looking here at citations of philosophy journal articles in philosophy journal articles, so it doesn't capture articles that get a lot of uptake outside philosophy.

For that reason, I think it's a pretty good guide to what was being talked about in philosophy departments over time.

Certainly the values for imagined researchers in the 1990s match up with the sense I got as a grad student of what were the big topics.

At the time I thought Real Philosophers did philosophy of mind, especially mental content, and since I had nothing to say about that, it wasn't great for me.

In the full post I record not just what the top article was by this measure at different times, but what the top tens were. They don't really change the broad picture that you get from this list of what was at the center of the conversations, but they are useful to see.

I also list which articles have been most cited (in philosophy journals) since 2020 but which were not on these top ten lists.

That list is more diverse both in subject matter and authorship than the earlier lists, which I think says something good about the direction philosophy is going.
