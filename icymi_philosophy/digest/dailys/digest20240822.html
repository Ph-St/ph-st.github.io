Kent Pitman

@kentpitman@climatejustice.social

We expect global warming to be uniform hiking of temperatures, but we don't always get or perceive that. The reason scientists speak of global average temperature is that the effects are more varied at individual points around he globe.

In addition to more heat, we get more volatility, extremes of heat and cold. Overall trend is warmer, on average, but in the micro, at any particular point, things are more varied.

Meanwhile seasons change, and we shift in some places from risk of extreme heat to risk of extreme cold. In other places we just have warmer winters. Or maybe it's easier for us to perceive later winters and earlier summers. If we ignore the gestalt science tries to offer, we are reduced to such visceral and anecdotal asessments.

The haiku plays with this transition by noting how the overt signs, the ones that drive our bodies' visceral reactions rather than the ones that drive our science, appear to waver, to become less pronounced and less certain. Our intuitions are wrong, says the science, but it still has subtle effects on our sense if urgency.

The haiku plays with "sign waver" and "sins waiver". For non-native English speakers, waver without an "i" means wobble and with an "i" means a kind of formal cancellation, so an apparent absolving of sin is going on, seasonally at least.

Though not spelled out, there is a third meaning, packed in as a pun in the third line, a pun about a sine wave, representing the seasonal shift. Instead of climate sin waiver, so you can alternatively read sin as sine there: sine waiver. It's up to you whether this means a sibe wave implements a waiver, or whether it means sine waver, as if referring to some initiating entity that is waving back and forth to create a sin wave itself. Haikus have little room, so one sometimes works to compress meaning into them.

Or one must let go of precsion and just, yeah ride the waves of impression. The signs are all there and it's up to you what to make if them.

But my political point, my point about human survival, is that now that extremes of heat seasonally lessen, at least in the northern hemisphere, we'll go into coma, where urgency of climate change action is dormant another year, squandering valuable time to act that we cannot get back.And meanwhile the global average heat rises, so when we emerge from our coma next spring, it'll all be worse.

The Southern Hemisphere is the same seasonal cycle, but a half year out of phase, of course so they're coming out of their climate urgency coma as we enter ours.

And, right now in particular, as we each transition from high to low, both hemispheres hovering around the y-axis of that sin wave, we're momentarily each at a blah point that no one reports on as striking temperature-wise, even though it might be the time when the rise in local average annual temperature is most easily seen. It won't be, though. If there is peak anything in fall or spring, it's probably peak climate disinterest.

#climate #ClimateDenial #ClimateCrisis #seasons
