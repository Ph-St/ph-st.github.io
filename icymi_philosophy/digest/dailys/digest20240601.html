Leading into sentencing in Trump's trial, thinking about damages, I hope that not just monetary but non-monetary damages are taken into proper consideration.

He hid key info voters needed to make their election choice. It's easy to see how that could have affected the election outcome. Really that seemed his clear intent. It's therefore hard to imagine a more serious fraud, a fraud for which he shows no remorse and clear signs he'd do it again.

Moreover, frauds don't just have direct consequences, but indirect ones. NY fraud law says it's worse if you intend a crime. What made this such a big deal is that he wanted a win by illegal means. One consequence of such an ill-gotten win was that We The People ended up enduring an anti-science Covid policy that led to huge numbers of unnecessary deaths by letting things spread, by failing to socialize good hygiene, by making false statements, and so on.

There are many people now gone who might still be alive and well today but for the election outcome, so if this fraud even might have affected that, and he intended such an effect, then it's reasonable to see this as the most serious of frauds, calling for the most serious of sentences.

I'm not singling him out on this. I consider all election-related crime to be much more serious than we presently acknowledge, much more akin to treason. We fear military attacks on our country, but if someone does something even some apparently small as intentionally tell someone the wrong day to vote, or tries to convince people they cannot legally vote, they are potentially affecting the outcome. It differs from a military attack in mechanism, but not in political effect. Each potentially changes who will be ruling us. That is a BiG deal.

In my view, all election-related crime needs much more serious treatment than we customarily give it, or else our democracy will soon fall. We need to outlaw gerrymandering. We need to reinstate the voting rights that have been lost.

We need to eliminate this bogus rule -- not even a law -- that all hope of trials goes away as elections get close. The public needs to know whether the candidates they're voting on are criminals. We The People have a right to not just a speedy trial, but a "speedy enough" trial. Everyone knows that if Trump gets elected, he will work diligently to end prosecutions against that.

We discuss that with a kind of resigned sadness. But we must maintain not just a sense of outrage but a sense that this is a serious problem we must fix. These are attack surfaces for those who mean democracy to fall.

So there's a lot to be done. But the most immediate thing is sentencing. This crime was about hiding what voters needed to know going into an election, with a material chance of affecting the outcome, so it must not be seen as something for which there were no damages. It should be treated as extremely serious.

If we do not start taking these things seriously, those who would end democracy will have all the tools they need to dismantle it because they will know we are not really serious about protecting it.

We must change not only the law, but the mindset. These are not just trivia to discuss, but to-do items to attend to. Working together, with a clear sense of their importance, maybe we can do something. But shrugging it off as vague and probably unimportant will not accomplish that.

If the law is not clear enough that these vague things matter, let's fix that, too.
