The famous "veil of ignorance" idea proposes that when you can't consider what you would gain or lose from a decision, you will be more incentivized to consider fairness.

Sure enough, 5 experiments found that a veil of ignorance decision environment increased "preference[s] for a[n] #AI assistant to prioritize the worst-off".

This wasn't well explained by politics or the type of game use to elicit the decisions.
