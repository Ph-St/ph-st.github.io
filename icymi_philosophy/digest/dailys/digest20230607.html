CJ Stevens - Metaphysiology

@WorldImagining@mastodon.social

Why do #instrumentalism/ #experimentalism/ #pragmatism paradoxically get us closer to reality than #realism?

The former present themselves as a set of quests for meaning, ever-evolving, whereas the latter presents itself as a quest for final truths.

Meaning, as a concept, can never be fully mind-independent. Truth can claim to be. Realism bases its claim to certainty upon mind-independence. However, certainty is itself a meaning. Certainty only has meaning in minds, for agents.

I keep seeing a sort of performance-function fallacy in AI discourse.

As I say in this piece, human-level performance doesn't entail human-level reasoning, understanding, or function.

Just like we can fake our way through tasks by mimicking our observations of competent people in similar situations, AI can use statistical associations to generate a mimic of what it has observed in the relevant parts of its training data. Mimicry doesn't always require understanding.
