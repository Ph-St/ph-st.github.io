Today I brushed up on one of the funnier counterarguments in philosophy.

There's a view called the Equal Weight View which says that if you and an epistemic peer disagree about something, you ought to give their opinion equal weight to your own, and so you should give up your belief and become agnostic until further investigation breaks the impasse.

But philosophers disagree about whether the Equal Weight View is true. So defenders of the Equal Weight View ought to give up on their view.
