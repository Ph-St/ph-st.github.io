"Those homeless people," said the man at the bar to his assembled friends, almost as if the noun phrase was a complete thought that required no verb or adjectives. But he clarified anyway. "They are leaches on society."

I sipped my diet soda thoughtfully, contemplating the vast resources that people living on the streets with barely a possession to their name take from us.

The man went on proudly pontificating to his friends. HE had a job. Never mind that the man's job did not provide anything society needed in order to sustain itself.

Never mind that going to and from that job burned fossil fuels, spewing CO2 into the atmosphere. Never mind that since no one wanted that product without coaxing, someone else would have to get in their fossil-fuel-burning car and drive to their job to help sell the unnecessary product to be delivered by Amazon using planes and other vehicles. Not to mention the supply chain for the parts of that product.

Never mind that the man's job earned him enough to buy heating oil that would spew more CO2.

Never mind that he regularly flew places for work or for vacations, and ordered from Amazon. He PAID for his lifestyle. Not like these people who had no car, no heat, no vacations, no Amazon deliveries.

"If they'd had jobs like mine," the man said, as I in my head injected "that cause active damage to the environment". He couldn't hear me though and continued, "Then I'd help them--of course I'd help them--get back to that important business."

"But these people--", he went on, "these homeless people," he said, clarifying. "If I gave them money," the man said, as if to seal the deal, "they'd probably spend it on alcohol." The others assembled nodded agreement and clinked glasses before ordering up another drink.

After further mulling, I found myself agreeing that, perhaps just a bit, people who take from the world in a way that doesn't balance might well be a serious problem. It's just ... well, ...

I'm just not sure I agree with that smugly self-assured guy about WHO is the real drain in that picture he was painting.
