Brian Weatherson

The differences between the European and US academic job markets seem to be expanding.

At Michigan, like I think most places in the US, people apply for jobs in the Fall of the last year of their PhD, then turn in the dissertation in the spring. So people going on the job market now are 6 months from finishing.

But I've seen a few European jobs advertised in the last few weeks that require the PhD in hand at time of application. That rules out US applicants - unless they applied for jobs last year and struck out.

I don't know whether it would be better to do things the USian way or the European way, but there is a coordination problem here that I think we're collectively failing.
