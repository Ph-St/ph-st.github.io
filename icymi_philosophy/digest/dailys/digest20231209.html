@PatternChaser @analyticus @philosophy @philosophie @philosophyofmind It will always be inappropriately used in some domains, including reality.

But the real problem is that deduction is wildly overrated.
People think it's infallible, and provides capital-t True conclusions.

But mostly it provides nothing of value. An invalid argument has zero evidential value. An unsound argumetn has zero evidential value.

Meanwhile, a bigger problem: What we actually use is, as Hume showed, NOT deduction. What we actually use is far more robust, and far more powerful. And because people don't pay attention to it, they never learn to beware the specific drawbacks it has.

When these drawbacks are weaponized with deductive trappings, things get very bad.
